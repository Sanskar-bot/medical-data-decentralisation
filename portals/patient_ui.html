About MedVault ℹ️

Built for patients. Secured by cryptography. Controlled by you.

🏥
MedVault
A decentralised, patient-first medical data platform. Your health records are encrypted on your device — no cloud, no third-party, no data mining. Doctors access your data only when you explicitly approve it.
🎯 What is MedVault?

MedVault is a decentralised health data management system that puts patients in complete control of their medical records. Unlike traditional hospital systems where your data lives on centralized servers accessible to anyone with admin rights, MedVault stores your encrypted data on your own device. Only you hold the keys.

🛡 Security Features
🔑
RSA-2048 Asymmetric Encryption
A unique RSA keypair is generated locally on your device during registration. Your private key never leaves your machine. The public key is used by doctors to wrap session keys securely.
🔐
AES-256-GCM Data Encryption
All medical records are encrypted with AES-256-GCM before storage. Each record has its own unique encryption key, which is itself wrapped with your password-derived key.
🧂
PBKDF2 Password-Based Key Derivation
Your password is never stored. It's transformed using PBKDF2 with a random salt into a Key Encryption Key (KEK) that unlocks your record key locally. Brute-forcing is computationally infeasible.
✍️
Digital Signatures (RSA + SHA-256)
Every record is signed with your private key. This ensures data integrity — any tampering with your encrypted record is detectable because the signature will fail verification.
🎟
JWT Authentication (HS256)
Session tokens are signed JWTs with a 15-minute expiry. Refresh tokens last 7 days. Failed logins are tracked and accounts are locked after 5 incorrect attempts.
📲
QR Code Access Flow
Doctors present a QR code. You scan it and decrypt access using your own password. Your data key is re-wrapped with the doctor's public key, giving them a 24-hour time-limited, patient-authorised session.
📋
Immutable Audit Log
Every login, access request, approval, denial, note addition, and data fetch is logged with a timestamp and actor. You can view the full history at any time in the Audit Log tab.
🚦
Rate Limiting & Account Lockout
API endpoints are rate-limited to prevent brute-force attacks. Accounts are automatically locked after 5 consecutive failed login attempts and require admin intervention to unlock.
